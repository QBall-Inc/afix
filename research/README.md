# AFIX Research

This directory contains the full evidentiary chain behind the AFIX governance schema proposal: protocol surface analysis, schema design decisions, stress-tests, steelmanned counterarguments, and pilot design.

## What This Contains

| Directory | Contents |
|-----------|----------|
| `synthesis/` | Protocol governance surface analysis (B.2.1) and schema design decisions (B.2.2) |
| `stress-tests/` | Assumptions stress-test (Phase A), steelmanned counterarguments (Phase B), and hostile reviewer pass (Phase C) |
| `decisions/` | Design decisions with rationale and external paper comparison |
| `pilot/` | ACGP three-firm pilot architecture with measurable success criteria |
| `landscape/` | Industry landscape analysis — governance capabilities shipping from Google Cloud, Oracle, AWS, Stripe, and Cloudflare (April 2026) |

## Research Methodology

Research was conducted using five sequential roles: **Context Establisher** (scoped the protocol landscape and research boundaries), **Source Investigator** and **Landscape Analyst** (ran in parallel across MCP, A2A, AP2/UCP, and Visa TAP primary sources), **Design Exerciser** (produced the schema components and binding specifications), and **Stress-Tester** (ran three adversarial passes: assumption stress-testing, steelmanned counterarguments, and a hostile senior-practitioner review).

## Relationship to the AFIX Schemas

The research documents establish the evidentiary foundation for schema design choices. Synthesis documents explain what the protocols provide natively. Stress-test documents record which design assumptions held, which were revised, and which remain open. Decision documents trace specific field-level choices to their rationale and sources.

## Source Tiering

All sources are tiered T1–T4 per the following evidence hierarchy:

- **T1** — Primary sources: official protocol specifications, regulatory texts, company announcements
- **T2** — Secondary analysis: analyst reports, vendor white papers with primary backing
- **T3** — Tertiary: industry surveys, aggregated market data
- **T4** — Practitioner observation: directional signals, unverified practitioner accounts
